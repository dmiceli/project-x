# MARKETING.md — ASO + launch plan (draft for Dan's review)

*Researched 2026-07-02. Reality check up front: organic virality on TikTok is harder than in 2023 — meaningful reach now often needs paid boost, and the algorithm rewards niche consistency over one-off clips ([Cloutboost 2026](https://www.cloutboost.com/blog/tiktoks-changing-landscape-for-game-marketing-in-2026-what-developers-need-to-know), [FluxNote](https://fluxnote.io/blog/tiktok-strategy-for-gaming-creators-in-2026)). Plan accordingly: the share card inside the game is our scalable channel; social content is the amplifier, not the engine.*

## App Store Optimization

**Title:** "TAKE 47" alone is short and brandable but keyword-empty. App Store allows a 30-char title — use it: **"TAKE 47 — Stunt Flip Game"** (verify final phrasing against keyword research at submission). **Subtitle (30 chars):** sells the joke + genre: e.g., "Land the stunt. Or don't." **Keyword field (100 chars, invisible to users):** flip, stunt, backflip, ragdoll, one tap, tricky, hard game, funny, physics, arcade — refine against competitor spy tools later. **Category:** Games > Casual (secondary: Action). **Screenshots:** first two do all the work — one mid-flip with "TAP TO STOP THE SPIN", one CUT! screen with a director quip and a big take counter. **App preview video:** 15-second loop of jump → crash → quip → retry → perfect. The fail must be in the video; the fail is the ad.

## The built-in channel: share cards

Every CUT! screen and milestone offers a one-tap share image (scene number, take count, quip). This is the mechanism that scales with players instead of with our marketing time. Launch copy on the card should be a challenge, not a boast: "Scene 137. Take 1,048. Your turn." Card design gets its own polish pass in Phase 4 — it's a marketing asset, not an afterthought.

## TikTok / Shorts / Reels (organic, low-cost)

One account, one niche, relentlessly consistent: *the world's unluckiest stunt double*. Post the game's own comedy — best crashes, absurd quip pairings, "take 300 on scene 40" despair arcs. Formats that outperform in 2026: genuine-reaction facecam over gameplay (2-5x engagement vs gameplay-only) and single-tip micro-clips 15-20s ([Viryze](https://viryze.com/blog/tiktok-gaming-trends-2026)). Cadence goal: 3-4 clips/week for the 4 weeks around launch; measure, don't spray. Budget option later: Spark Ads boosting the organically-best clip rather than made-for-ads creative ([MegaDigital](https://megadigital.ai/en/blog/tiktok-ads-trends-for-mobile-games/)).

## TestFlight: getting to 20+ testers

Wave 1 (~10): friends/family/coworkers via direct TestFlight public link — zero friction, they'll forgive rough edges. Wave 2 (10-30): communities — r/iosgaming and r/AppleArcade beta threads, r/playmygame, r/TestFlight, TouchArcade forums beta section, indie-game Discords. Post format that works: one funny GIF of a crash, one sentence of pitch, public TestFlight link, explicit ask ("brutal feedback on difficulty wanted"). Offer credit in-game ("Stunt Crew" screen) — cheap, motivating. Wave 2 posts double as soft marketing; check each community's self-promo rules first.

## Press / creators (modest expectations)

Short list at launch: TouchArcade, PocketGamer, AppSpy successors, and 5-10 small mobile-gaming TikTok/YouTube creators (micro-creators respond far more often than big ones). One-paragraph pitch + presskit page (screenshots, GIFs, icon, TestFlight/App Store link). Expected hit rate is low; cost is one evening.

## Sequencing

Beta (TestFlight waves, gather quotes/clips) → polish → App Store launch with ASO in place → 4-week content push → evaluate paid boost only if a clip shows organic legs. Launch is not one day; it's the 6 weeks around approval.
