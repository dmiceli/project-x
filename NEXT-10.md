# NEXT-10.md — the next ten must-haves, and the three things that could sink us

*Overnight deep-dive, 2026-07-04 (pre-beta). Method: market research on the one-tap/flappy class, viral-simple-game postmortems, 2026 retention data, and the Wordle case; then every finding tested against what TAKE 47 already has and the standing principles (fully offline, ads never buy progress, earned-only economy). Sources at bottom. Verdicts are Claude's; decisions are Dan's.*

## What the research says, compressed

The Flappy class wins on simplicity + cathartic failure + frictionless sharing — the share button after every crash was load-bearing, and streamers were the actual distribution channel ([The Conversation](https://theconversation.com/flappy-bird-didnt-fail-it-was-a-classic-catastrophic-success-23331), [MarTech](https://martech.org/viral-rise-fall-flappy-bird/)). In 2026, competitive leaderboards are the single strongest retention correlate; streaks/achievements lift DAU ~12.7%; day-7-weighted reward curves drive commitment; players commit to fewer games but go deeper ([Game Growth Advisor](https://gamegrowthadvisor.com/blog/2026-03-17-mobile-game-retention-strategies-2026/), [maf.ad](https://maf.ad/en/blog/mobile-gaming-statistics/)). Wordle's engine was scarcity (one/day), a worldwide shared ritual, and a spoiler-free emoji share with no link — pull, not push ([Enchant](https://www.enchant.com/lessons-from-wordle), [Webflow](https://webflow.com/blog/wordle-design)). Indie launches die of invisibility in ~72 hours, not of quality; the winners arrive with an audience already built ([PixelPicked](https://pixelpicked.com/blogs/why-indie-mobile-games-keep-dying), [Metricus](https://metricusapp.com/blog/indie-game-distribution-user-acquisition-painpoints-2025-2026/)). And on TikTok, funny-fail gaming clips of 15–30s are a proven organic genre — a game that *manufactures* funny fails is a content factory ([Clypse](https://clypse.ai/blog/best-tiktok-gaming-clip-length-2026)).

TAKE 47's position: the core loop, daily ritual (same slate worldwide), fail-comedy identity, and share cards already exist. The gaps are competition visibility, streak structure, clip-ability, and everything to do with being found.

## The next 10 (ranked by impact-per-effort, dependencies noted)

**1 · Game Center daily leaderboard** *(already approved — this research re-confirms it as #1)*. The daily gimmick + same-slate-worldwide is our Wordle ritual; a leaderboard makes the ritual visible. Strongest retention correlate in the data. Apple-native: no backend, no privacy change. Wrapper work, ~1 session.

**2 · Spoiler-free emoji share for the daily (the Wordle grid).** Text, not image: `TAKE 47 · JUL 4 · UNDERCRANKED — 🎬🟨🟨🟥 · 3 scenes`. Text shares outtravel images in iMessage/group chats, carry no spoilers, and stack with the existing BEAT THIS card (image for socials, text for threads). Tiny scope, disproportionate viral surface. Design note: like Wordle, consider *no URL* in the text — mystique pulls harder than links.

**3 · Shooting-streak system.** Consecutive days on set, with a day-7-weighted reward curve (research: exponential curves drive the commitment psychology) and a "director's grace" freeze earned by perfect days — never bought (principle intact). Builds on the existing call sheet; the midnight notification (queued) becomes its heartbeat. ~1 session.

**4 · Outtake instant replay → clip export (the thesis, made literal).** Phase A: post-crash REPLAY button — cinematic letterbox, slate overlay ("TAKE 23 — OUTTAKE"), slow-mo at impact. Phase B: export as video (canvas.captureStream → file share; WKWebView support needs a spike — flag as research task). Fail clips are the organic-growth genre; every crash is 15 seconds of content. Phase A ~1 session; Phase B unknown, spike first.

**5 · First-session difficulty instrumentation-by-hand + adaptive mercy.** We have zero telemetry *by design*, so the only difficulty data we will ever get is watching humans. Formalize it: a beta observation protocol (watch 3 people, note quit-points), then an adaptive kindness — after N consecutive crashes on one scene, the tolerance quietly widens a degree with a director quip ("The director lowers his standards. Temporarily."). The 60%-quit-when-hard number is the retention floor threat. ~1 session after beta data.

**6 · Shooting-week arc on the call sheet.** Seven-day structure with a WEEK WRAPPED golden-poster payoff (day-7 weighting again), visible progress strip on the home screen. Pairs with #3. Small.

**7 · Signature audio sting + crash foley pass.** Clips (#4) are only funny with audible comedy — a recognizable 1-second crash sting is our audio logo (think Wilhelm scream economics). Current synth foley is solid; this is one iconic moment, not a soundtrack. Small scope, compounding brand value.

**8 · App Store product-page conversion kit.** Screenshots that read as comedy beats (verdict stamps, director quips, DAILIES REPORT), a 15–30s vertical preview that is literally an outtake montage, subtitle/keywords from MARKETING.md. The research is unambiguous that the page must convert the few who arrive. No code; one dedicated session with the existing brand kit.

**9 · Pre-launch audience infrastructure.** The TestFlight public link needs somewhere to live: a one-page site on Pages (playable demo IS the site — nobody else can do that), waitlist mail-to, 3 seeded outtake clips for TikTok/Shorts, and a shortlist of micro-streamers who cover rage/one-tap games (the PewDiePie lesson at achievable scale). This is the direct answer to the #1 killer below. Ongoing, starts pre-submission.

**10 · Accessibility differentiator pass.** One-tap games are uniquely positioned to be genuinely playable by more people than any other genre — we're already color-safe and reduce-motion-clean; add full VoiceOver landing announcements, a one-switch mode audit, and *say so on the store page*. Rare, cheap for us specifically, genuinely good, and App Store editorial notices accessibility stories. ~1 session.

**Considered and rejected:** battle pass / VIP club (violates the soul of "earned, never bought"), widgets (post-1.0 novelty), App Clips demo (heavy; Pages link already does this job), more sets (six is a launch-strength roster; content breadth isn't the constraint), any backend (privacy posture is a feature).

## The three things that sink us

**1 · Nobody ever sees it.** Not a risk — the statistical default. Hundreds of launches die weekly within 72 hours, invisible; charts, search, and featuring are all closed doors without either budget or pre-built audience. Our goals (approval + 20 testers) are honest, but if success quietly re-defines itself as "viral hit" without distribution work (#8, #9), the gap between effort and outcome kills projects — and morale. The game will not market itself; virality is a lottery and we've only designed the ticket. **Counter: treat #9 as a workstream equal to code, starting now; keep the definition of success written down.**

**2 · The first-session filter, which we are blind to by design.** Difficulty is tuned by the two people on earth least able to judge it, ~60% of players quit games that feel too hard too fast, and our zero-telemetry privacy posture — a genuine feature — means *no dashboard will ever warn us*. If scenes 1–10 shed most normal humans, every retention feature above is irrelevant. **Counter: the beta observation protocol (#5), the cold-reader audit, adaptive mercy, and treating tonight's friends & family as the most important data this project will ever collect.**

**3 · Apple Guideline 4.3 ("spam") / minimum-functionality rejection.** The one-tap genre is exactly where App Review swings the 4.3 hammer — thousands of flappy-clones taught them to. We are far past "clone" (six mechanics, daily system, economy, original art), but a tired reviewer sees "one-tap flip game" first. **Counter: the review notes must lead with depth (daily gimmick system, six distinct mechanics, accessibility work); screenshots must look like a *produced* game; the QA-panel strip gate matters here too — hidden test menus invite rejection; and if rejected, 4.3 is appealable with a differentiation write-up, which DESIGN.md basically already is.**

*(Honorable mention, because it sinks more indie projects than all three combined: the solo-owner marathon. Sprint pace built this in 72 hours; App Store ops, beta triage, marketing, and a day job are a different sport. The fixed submission date and the weekly case-study cadence are the guardrails — keep them.)*
