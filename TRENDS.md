# TRENDS.md — market trend analysis (2026-07-02)

## The headline: hyper-casual died, hybrid-casual ate it

The pure hyper-casual model (one mechanic, ad-only monetization, disposable players) has structurally declined; the industry's biggest hyper-casual publishers (Voodoo, CrazyLabs, Supersonic) have publicly pivoted to **hybrid-casual**: a simple casual core loop *plus* progression meta-layers, monetized by ads *and* IAP ([PocketGamer.biz](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/what-happened-to-hypercasual-the-markets-evolution-over-the-past-year/), [Deconstructor of Fun](https://www.deconstructoroffun.com/blog/2026/2/2/state-of-mobile-2026)).

The numbers behind the shift: hybrid-casual IAP revenue grew ~100% YoY in Q2 2025 (top-10 titles: $126M in one quarter); D7 retention runs 18-22% for hybrid vs 6-9% for hyper; D30 is 8-12% vs 2-4%; blended ARPDAU is 4-7× higher ($0.15-0.50 vs $0.03-0.08) ([Antier](https://www.antiersolutions.com/blogs/hybrid-casual-games-vs-hypercasual-whats-driving-higher-retention-ltv-and-revenue-in-2026/), [Game Growth Advisor](https://gamegrowthadvisor.com/blog/2026-04-16-hybrid-casual-game-design-strategy-2026/), [Tenjin benchmark](https://tenjin.com/blog/ad-mon-gaming-2026/)).

## What this means for TAKE 47

**We're accidentally on-trend — by design decisions already made.** A one-tap core loop with persistent scene progression, collectible characters, achievements, and a daily challenge is *precisely* the hybrid-casual formula. Dan's instinct to add levels ("further sense of accomplishment") is the same instinct the entire industry converged on. No design change needed; this analysis validates the current spec.

**Retention targets worth adopting** (post-launch measurement via Apple Analytics): D7 ≥ 15% would put us well above hyper-casual class; D30 ≥ 8% is hybrid-casual territory. The mechanisms that drive those numbers — daily challenge (comeback reason), scene progress (sunk-cost pull), character milestones (next-goal visibility) — are all in the v1 spec.

**Monetization implication** (BACKLOG, not v1): hybrid-casual revenue skews 40-60% IAP. Our v1 IAP is only remove-ads. If retention proves out, the natural post-launch addition is cosmetic character IAP (premium skins that don't touch progression — consistent with the ads-never-buy-progress principle). Logged in BACKLOG.md.

**Ad-revenue realism:** ad-only hyper-casual ARPDAU of ~$0.03-0.08 means ads alone won't pay meaningfully without large volume. The share-card loop and retention meta aren't nice-to-haves; they're the revenue model.
