# THEME.md — movie-set flavor research (theme-within-the-theme)

*Researched 2026-07-02, for the art/writing passes. Feeds: scene visuals, quips, achievement names, share-card flavor.*

## IP guardrails (read first)

We never use real movie titles, character names, actor names, studio names, or quotable lines — all trademark/copyright exposure in a commercial App Store product. What IS safe and plentiful: **tropes** (genres, archetypes, situations aren't ownable), **film-industry jargon** (takes, prints, second unit, craft services — free vocabulary, deeply flavorful), and **history/homage in original words** (facts about stunt history are public; our jokes about them must be our own writing). Every reference below follows this rule.

## The gold mine: stunt history lore

Film history's stunt lore is inherently comedic and public-domain-friendly. Buster Keaton — Hollywood's original actor-stuntman — performed the most famous stunt ever filmed: a two-ton house facade falls around him, he survives by standing exactly where the open window lands, his position marked by a single nail ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steamboat_Bill,_Jr.), [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Buster-Keaton)). That *precision-or-death, marked-spot* idea is practically our game's mechanic — an homage scene type (facade falls behind the player as set dressing on a milestone scene) would be a deep-cut nod without referencing anything protected. General stunt-craft flavor for quips and achievements: crash mats, airbags, squibs, wire teams, "the double does it in one," the tradition that stunt performers never say "good luck."

## Scene genres — the design unlock

As scenes climb, the *set changes genre* every ~10-15 scenes: backlot western (saloon, tumbleweed), swashbuckler (rigging, masts), 50s sci-fi (cardboard flying saucer on strings), kaiju/monster (miniature city, rubber-suit arm in frame), noir (rain, neon, venetian-blind light), space opera, wuxia wire-work, found-footage horror (shaky handheld vignette). All trope-level, zero licensed content, huge visual variety from palette+props swaps on the same cached-set system. The BIG STUNT every 10th scene becomes that genre's finale. This also gives progression a *reason to see what's next* — a proven retention hook that costs only background art.

## Director quips by archetype

Instead of one director, quips can rotate through recognizable-but-original archetypes: the tortured auteur ("This scene is a metaphor. You've ruined the metaphor."), the budget producer ("That crash cost forty thousand dollars. Do it cheaper."), the over-it veteran ("In my day we landed twice on Sundays."), the film-school intern ("Um. The light was, um, perfect, and you were... not."). Archetypes are tropes: safe, extensible, and they set up quip packs per genre block (the western scenes get a western director).

## Achievement/copy flavor pulled from industry jargon

"Second Unit" (complete 5 scenes without a perfect), "Craft Services" (return after a break), "Day Player" (first session), "The Nail" (land a perfect on a facade-fall homage scene), "Wrap Party" (finish a genre block). All original phrasing, all industry-flavored.
